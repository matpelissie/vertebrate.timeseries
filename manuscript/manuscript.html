Introduction

According to the latest WWF report, the average population size of terrestrial vertebrates has fallen by 68% In less than 50 years(WWF, 2020). However, recent studies have even suggested that the dynamics of biodiversity are not really declining on average. They postulate that species at risk are dragging down global indices (Leung et al., 2020), but that there are as many âwinnersâ as âlosersâ species in total (Dornelas et al., 2019). We also aim to investigate whether population dynamics are influenced by climate change.

Population data

The database considered contains 1212 species of terrestrial vertebrates and 5529 population time series distributed worldwide. We only focused on time series beginning after 1980 in order to be consistent with the climate data we analyse in the following part.

Each population time series was fitted with a linear regression model to quantify the trend through:
